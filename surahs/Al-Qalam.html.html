SURAH AL-QALAM · ٱلْقَلَم

The Pen — A Defense and a Reminder

Surah Al-Qalam is one of the very earliest Makkan surahs — the second revealed after Surah Al-‘Alaq. It opens with a divine oath by the Pen, defends the Prophet ﷺ against the accusation of madness, and praises him with the highest commendation in the Qur'an: "Indeed you are upon an exalted standard of character." It then exposes nine vices of the proud denier, narrates the parable of the owners of the garden who forgot to say "in shaa Allah", contrasts believers with criminals on the Day the Shin is uncovered, and reminds the Prophet ﷺ to be patient — without hastening as Yunus عليه السلام hastened.
