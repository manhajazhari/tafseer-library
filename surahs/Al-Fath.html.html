SURAH AL-FATH · ٱلْفَتْح

The Victory — A Manifest Opening at Hudaybiyyah

Surah Al-Fath is a Madani surah revealed in Dhul-Qa'dah of 6 AH on the road back from the Treaty of Hudaybiyyah. To the eye the believers had been turned back from 'umrah; to Allah the treaty was 'a manifest opening' — the gate that opened to the Conquest of Khaybar, the entry of Arab tribes into Islam, and the Conquest of Makkah two years later. The surah honours the Bay'ah of Ridwan, names the lagging Bedouins, defends the believing men and women hidden in Makkah, confirms the Prophet's true dream, and closes with the most luminous portrait of the Companions in the Qur'an.
