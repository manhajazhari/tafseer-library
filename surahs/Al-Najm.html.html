SURAH AN-NAJM · ٱلنَّجْم

The Star — Defense, Ascension, and the Closing Sajdah

Surah An-Najm is one of the very earliest Makkan surahs. It opens with a divine oath by the descending star, defends the Prophet ﷺ — "your companion has not strayed nor erred" — and certifies revelation: "Nor does he speak from his own desire." It then ascends with him to the Sidratul-Muntahaa, demolishes the three chief idols (Al-Laat, Al-‘Uzza, Manaat), recalls the eternal principles from the scrolls of Musa and Ibrahim, names the destroyed nations of ‘Aad, Thamud, Nuh and Lut, and closes with the first public sajdah of recitation ever recited openly in the Sacred Mosque.
