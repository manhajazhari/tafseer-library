SURAH AL-MA'ARIJ · ٱلْمَعَارِج

The Ways of Ascent — Patience, the Halu' Soul, and the Cure of Salah

Surah Al-Ma'arij is a late Makkan surah of 44 verses. It opens with a mocking question of an-Nadr ibn al-Harith about an inevitable punishment, ascends to the Day of Fifty Thousand Years, stages the failed ransom of the criminal who would trade his children — wife — brother — clan — even all humanity to escape Lazaa, diagnoses the human soul as halu' (anxious, fretful), and prescribes the cure in twelve verses: the profile of the musalleen, bracketed at both ends by salah. It closes by ordering the Prophet ﷺ to leave the mockers of Quraysh to their play — until the Day they race from their graves to the promised Reckoning.
