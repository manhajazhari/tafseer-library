SURAH AL-INSAN · ٱلْإِنسَٰن

The Human — A Surah of the Abrar

Surah Al-Insan is a Madani surah of 31 verses, also known as Surah Ad-Dahr and Hal Ataa. It opens with the deepest humility — reminding man that he was once "nothing worth mentioning" — then names his creation from a mingled drop, his equipping with hearing and sight, and his single great choice: grateful or denier. The Surah's heart is the portrait of the Abrar (the righteous) — those who fulfil vows, fear the heavy Day, and feed the poor, orphan, and captive while loving the food themselves, saying: "We want from you neither reward nor thanks." Their reward unfolds in layered detail: a Garden of silk, low-hanging fruits, silver vessels clear as crystal, springs of Kafur, Zanjabeel, and Salsabeel, and the climax — "their Lord shall give them to drink a pure drink." The Surah closes with the Prophet's daily programme — dhikr at dawn and afternoon, sujood and long tasbeeh through the night — and the perfect Sunni balance: "whoever wills, let him take a way to his Lord — and you do not will unless Allah wills."
