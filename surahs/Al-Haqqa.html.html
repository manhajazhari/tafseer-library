SURAH AL-HAAQQAH · ٱلْحَآقَّة

The Inevitable Reality — A Surah of Awe

Surah Al-Haaqqah is a powerful Makkan surah of 52 verses, named after the Day of Resurrection — "the Inevitable Reality" that makes truth prevail. It opens with a triple magnification of that Day, recounts the destruction of Thamud, 'Ad, Pharaoh, and the people of Lut, then carries us through Nuh's Ark, the blowing of the Trumpet, the splitting of the sky, and the eight angels who bear the Throne. It contrasts the joyful believer holding his record in his right hand with the despairing denier chained seventy cubits. It seals with one of the strongest defenses of the Qur'an in the entire Book, closing on the command: "So glorify the name of your Lord, the Most Great."
