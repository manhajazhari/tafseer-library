SURAH AL-JATHIYAH · ٱلْجَاثِيَة

The Kneeling — Signs, Resurrection, and Glory

Surah Al-Jathiyah is a Makkan surah and the sixth of the seven Hawamim. It opens by naming the Book as a revelation from the Almighty, the All-Wise, then lays out three graded tiers of cosmic, biological, and meteorological signs for the believers, the certain, and the reasoners. It exposes the cultured denier who hears the verses then mocks them, refutes the materialist creed that nothing exists beyond this life and time, warns against taking one's desire as one's god, and culminates in the Great Scene that gives the surah its name — every nation kneeling before its book on the Day of Resurrection. It closes by returning to its opening: praise and majesty belong to Allah, al-'Azeez al-Hakeem.
