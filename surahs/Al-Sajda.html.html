SURAH AS-SAJDAH · ٱلسَّجْدَة

The Prostration — Tawḥīd, Creation, and the Return

Surah As-Sajdah is a Makkan surah of thirty verses, named after the prostration in verse 15. It opens by affirming that the Qur'an is undoubtedly from the Lord of the worlds, then turns the heart to three great signs: Allah's creation of the heavens and the earth in six days and His establishment over the Throne, the staged creation of the human being from clay then a drop of fluid then a soul, and the certainty of resurrection. It contrasts the people of the night — whose sides leave their beds to call upon their Lord — with the wrongdoers who turn away. The surah closes with a command to the Prophet ﷺ to be patient and to wait, for they too are waiting.
