SURAH AL-JINN · ٱلْجِنّ

The Jinn — Witnesses to the Qur'an

Surah Al-Jinn is a Makkan surah of 28 verses, revealed after the journey of Ta'if when the Prophet ﷺ recited the Qur'an at Nakhlah and a party of Jinn listened, were stunned by its truth, and returned to their people calling them to faith. The surah affirms pure Tawheed, exposes the foolishness of ascribing a consort or a child to Allah, refutes the pagans' refuge in the Jinn, narrates how the heavens are now guarded by shooting stars, and ends by reminding the Prophet ﷺ that he owns neither harm nor guidance — only the conveyance of the message — and that Allah alone knows the unseen.
