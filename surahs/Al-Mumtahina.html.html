SURAH AL-MUMTAHANAH · الممتحنة

Al-Mumtahanah — Loyalty to Allah, Disassociation from His Enemies

Surah al-Mumtahanah is a Madani surah of 13 verses, revealed after the truce of Hudaybiyyah and before the Conquest of Makkah. It opens with the story of Hatib ibn Abi Balta'a, who secretly sent word to Quraysh — and the surah forbids inward alliance with the enemies of the faith. It hands the believers the eternal example of Ibrahim ﷺ and his companions, opens the wide door of birr and qist toward peaceful non-Muslims (vv. 8–9), legislates the famous law of the migrating women (v.10, the verse that named the surah), and seals with Bay'at an-Nisa' on the Day of the Conquest — and a final warning against allying with people upon whom Allah is wrathful and who despair of the Hereafter.
