SURAH AL-AHQAF · ٱلْأَحْقَاف

Al-Ahqaf — The Sand-Dunes, Steadfastness, and the Call of Hud

Surah al-Ahqaf is a Makkan surah of 35 verses, named after the curved sand-dunes (al-Ahqaf) in verse 21 where the prophet Hud عليه السلام warned the tribe of 'Aad. It names the source of the Qur'an, lays out a four-fold rational challenge to shirk, gives the pillar verse of istiqaamah ('Our Lord is Allah, then remain steadfast'), the famous du'a of the forty-year-old ('Rabbi awzi'nee'), the warning to the son who says 'uff' to his parents, the destruction of 'Aad by the wind, and the honour of the night the jinn listened to the Qur'an — and closes with the command: be patient like Ulul-'Azm of the messengers.
