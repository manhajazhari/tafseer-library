SURAH AL-MUJADALAH · ٱلْمُجَادَلَة

The Pleading Woman — A Voice Heard from Above the Seven Heavens

Surah Al-Mujadalah is a Madinan surah named after Khawla bint Tha'laba, who came to the Prophet ﷺ pleading her case against her husband who had pronounced Zihar against her. Allah heard her from above the seven heavens and revealed the opening verses, abolishing the cruel pre-Islamic custom of Zihar. The surah then legislates the etiquette of private conversation (najwa), commands believers to make room for one another in gatherings, raises the rank of those given knowledge, exposes the hypocrites who befriend the enemies of Allah, and ends with the unforgettable contrast: the Party of Allah — they are the successful — versus the party of Shaytan — they are the losers.
