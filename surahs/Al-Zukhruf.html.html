SURAH AZ-ZUKHRUF · ٱلزُّخْرُف

Az-Zukhruf — Ornament Stripped, Truth Defended

Surah Az-Zukhruf is a Makkan surah of 89 verses, named after the word 'zukhruf' (ornament, gold) in verse 35. It defends the Arabic Qur'an, refutes the lie that angels are 'daughters of Allah', exposes blind imitation of ancestors, retells Ibrahim's eternal word of tawheed, tells the story of Musa with Pharaoh, settles the Qurayshi trap about Isa عليه السلام with one word — 'servant' — and closes with one of the most quoted promises in the Qur'an: 'O My servants! No fear shall be upon you this Day, nor shall you grieve.'
