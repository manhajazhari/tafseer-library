SURAH NUH · نُوح

Nuh — Nine and a Half Centuries of Da'wah and the Master Key of Istighfar

Surah Nuh is a Makkan surah of 28 verses dedicated entirely to one prophet: Nuh عليه السلام, who called his people for 950 years. The surah opens with the divine commission to warn before punishment, recounts Nuh's exhaustive da'wah — night and day, open and secret, public and private — and his despair as the people put their fingers in their ears and pulled their garments over their heads. It unveils istighfar as a master key that brings rain, wealth, sons, gardens, and rivers. It mounts a cosmic argument from the seven layered heavens, the moon as light and the sun as lamp, and human creation from earth. It names the five idols (Wadd, Suwa', Yaghuth, Ya'uq, Nasr) whose worship spread misguidance, ends with the Flood and the Fire as the verdict, and closes with Nuh's final du'a for the believers and against the disbelievers.
